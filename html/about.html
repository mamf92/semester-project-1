Our story

The Community Science Museum began as a dream shared by a small group of passionate scientists and educators. Founded in 1995 with just a handful of exhibits, it quickly became a place where visitors of all ages could explore the wonders of science.

The Community Science Museum began as a dream shared by a small group of passionate scientists and educators. Founded in 1995 with just a handful of exhibits, it quickly became a place where visitors of all ages could explore the wonders of science.

Over the years, we’ve grown into a thriving hub of discovery, with over 1,000 interactive exhibits showcasing everything from the tiniest particles to the vastness of space. Our collaborations with researchers and universities ensure that we remain at the forefront of scientific exploration.

At the heart of our story is a simple belief: science belongs to everyone. It’s not confined to labs or textbooks—it’s part of our daily lives. We’re proud to provide a space where curiosity is celebrated, and visitors are inspired to question, learn, and discover.

Our goals

The aim of our museum is to create a space where everyone can experience the wonders of our discoveries and perhaps even ignite a lifelong passion to continue the exploration of the world around us.

Over the course of human history, science has developed from our early understanding of fire, wind, water, and earth to exploring everything from galaxies far away to the very building blocks of life itself.

The aim of our museum is to create a space where everyone can experience the wonders of our discoveries and perhaps even ignite a lifelong passion to continue the exploration of the world around us.

We believe science should not be confined to the textbook, but brought to live through exhibits. This is why we have over 1000 different exhibits on the many varied subjects of science to explore. Many of these exhibits are designed for you to interact with and play around to see science come to life (apart from the dinosaur exhibits – they only come to life at night when everyone’s gone home).
